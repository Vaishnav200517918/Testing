Family Tree

V.R. Ajith Kumar

Human ancestry dates back to time immemorial and can be difficult to trace. An Indian or a Keralite today might have origins in the African continent or from a landmass that is now submerged or untraceable. It is best to document ancestry from the point where some knowledge is available. This attempt is made with that perspective in mind, aiming for a satisfactory account.

Ancestry: My ancestral family, to my knowledge, originated from Kochu Kaleekkal, Kallelibhagam, Karunagappally, Kollam, Kerala. It was primarily an agricultural family.

Maternal Lineage: My mother, P. Santhamma, is the daughter of K. Pankajakshi Amma of Kochu Kaleekkal House, Kallelibhagam, Thodiyur Panchayat, Karunagappally Taluk, Kollam District, and Kunjan Pillai of the Thuppadayyath family from the same place. Santhamma had a brother, K. Karunakaran Pillai, who was a traditional vaidyan and ran an Ayurvedic center and indigenous medicine shop.

Pankajakshi Amma’s (my grandmother’s) mother was Kalyani Amma, and her father was Govinda Pillai. Kalyani Amma belonged to the Kochu Kaleekkal family, and Govinda Pillai was from Perinad, Kollam. From Perinad, they moved to Panmana and later to Kallelibhagam. Kalyani Amma's mother was Nangeli Amma of the Kaleekkal family of Kallelibhagam.

Pankajakshi Amma had two sisters, Parvathi Amma and Lakshmi Amma, and four brothers: • Kunjan Pillai, who settled at Thodiyur Aikkara Padinjattathil • Raghavan Pillai, who settled at Mararithottam • Kuttan Pillai, who settled at Krishnapuram near Ochira • Chellappan Pillai, who settled at Kavanal Tharayil, Thodiyur

Paternal Lineage: My father, V. Raghavan Pillai, was a primary school teacher who retired as a Headmaster. He was actively involved in politics and teachers' organizations, including the Communist Party of India. After the party split, he initiated another organization, the Kerala Government Primary School Teachers' Union, serving as its General Secretary and President. Upon being promoted to Headmaster, he worked as the General Secretary and later as the President of the Kerala Government Primary School Headmasters' Association. After retirement, he served as a State-level Executive Member of the Kerala Land Mortgage Bank and a Director Board Member of the Karunagappally Land Mortgage Bank.

His father, Velayudhan Pillai, was from Valiyavilayil, South Mynagappally, Kollam, and his mother, Kalyani Amma, was also from South Mynagappally.

Present: V. Raghavan Pillai and P. Santhamma have three children:
V.R. Ajayakumar – The eldest son, after completing his education, joined Cochin Shipyard and retired in 2017. He settled in Kadavanthra, Kochi. His wife, Rema Devi, is from Mararithottam, Karunagappally. They have two sons, Srijesh Ajay and Sreeju Ajay, both working in the IT field. Both are married and have one child each.
V.R. Ajith Kumar – Worked in the Information and Public Relations Department of the Government of Kerala and retired in 2016. He married L. Jayasree from Valiya Nettavilayil, South Paravoor, Kollam. They have two children: • Asha Ajith – Joined the Indian Administrative Service after completing her education. She is married to Vishnu Chandran, also from the Indian Administrative Service, son of Balachandran and Geetha, a resident of Pachallur, Thiruvananthapuram. They have a daughter, Padmavathi. • Sreekuttan – Works as an engineer in a city gas distribution company. Their permanent residence is in Thiruvananthapuram.
K.S. Vineetha Kumari – Worked as a Mathematics teacher in a Government High School and retired from Chadayamangalam Government School. Her husband, B. Sasidharan Nair, is the son of Balakrishnan Nair and Rajamma from Kizhakkanela, Parippally. They had a son, Sambhu, who sadly passed away at the age of 21 after a prolonged illness. They are settled in Nilamel, Kollam.

L. Jayasree

Ancestry: Jayasree’s parents are from Paravoor. Her father, V. Gopinathan Pillai, was the Chief Officer of Indian Overseas Bank, and her mother, B. Lalitha Bai, is from Valiya Nettavilayil, the ancestral house where her sister Rajasree currently resides.

Lalitha Bai was the daughter of Karimpilangil Kesava Kurup, an agriculturalist and coir entrepreneur. She was the youngest of seven children. Her eldest brother, Surendran Pillai, was an agriculturalist, followed by her other brothers, Balachandran Pillai and Saratchandran Pillai.

Present: Jayasree has three sisters:

Rajasree – Unmarried and residing in the ancestral house.
Vijayasree – Works as a bank employee at Indian Overseas Bank. Her husband, Sajeev Kumar G.S., retired from the State Bank of Travancore and is a native of Mayyanad, Kollam. They have two sons: • Vaishak S.V. – Married to Pooja, and they are presently in Canada. • Vaishnav S.V. – Also studying in Canada.
Deepa Gopinath – A Senior Higher Secondary Mathematics teacher working in a Kerala Government School. Her husband, Shibu Anand, is an Assistant Professor of Economics in the Government sector. They have two daughters, Anagha and Ananya, both studying in school.
